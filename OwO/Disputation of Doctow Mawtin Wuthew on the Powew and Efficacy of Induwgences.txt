  DISPUTATION OF DOCTOW MAWTIN WUTHEW
  ON THE POWEW AND EFFICACY OF
  INDUWGENCES

OCTOBEW 31, 1517

Out of wove fow the twuth and the desiwe to bwing it to wight, the
fowwowing pwopositions wiww be discussed at Wittenbewg, undew the
pwesidency of the Wevewend Fathew Mawtin Wuthew, Mastew of Awts and of
Sacwed Theowogy, and Wectuwew in Owdinyawy on the same at that pwace. ^w^
Whewefowe he wequests that those who awe unyabwe to be pwesent and
debate owawwy with us, may do so by wettew. ^w^

In the Nyame ouw Wowd Jesus Chwist. Amen. >w<

1. (´・ω・\`) Ouw Wowd and Mastew Jesus Chwist, when He said Poenyitentiam agite,
wiwwed that the whowe wife of bewievews shouwd be wepentance. ;;w;;

2. This wowd cannyot be undewstood to mean sacwamentaw penyance, i.e. (´・ω・\`),
confession and satisfaction, which is adminyistewed by the pwiests. UwU

3. ;;w;; Yet it means nyot inwawd wepentance onwy; nyay, thewe is nyo inwawd
wepentance which does nyot outwawdwy wowk divews mowtifications of the
fwesh. UwU

4. ^w^ The penyawty [of sin], thewefowe, continyues so wong as hatwed of sewf
continyues; fow this is the twue inwawd wepentance, and continyues untiw
ouw entwance into the kingdom of heaven. >w<

5. UwU The pope does nyot intend to wemit, and cannyot wemit any penyawties
othew than those which he has imposed eithew by his own authowity ow by
that of the Canyons. (´・ω・\`)

6. UwU The pope cannyot wemit any guiwt, except by decwawing that it has
been wemitted by God and by assenting to God's wemission; though, to be
suwe, he may gwant wemission in cases wesewved to his judgment. (・`ω\´・) If his
wight to gwant wemission in such cases wewe despised, the guiwt wouwd
wemain entiwewy unfowgiven. ;;w;;

7. (・`ω\´・) God wemits guiwt to nyo onye whom He does nyot, at the same time,
humbwe in aww things and bwing into subjection to His vicaw, the
pwiest.  8. ^w^ The penyitentiaw canyons awe imposed onwy on the wiving, and,
accowding to them, nyothing shouwd be imposed on the dying. ;;w;;

9. ^w^ Thewefowe the Howy Spiwit in the pope is kind to us, because in his
decwees he awways makes exception of the awticwe of death and of
nyecessity. (・`ω\´・)

10. UwU Ignyowant and wicked awe the doings of those pwiests who, in the
case of the dying, wesewve canyonyicaw penyances fow puwgatowy. ^w^

11. UwU This changing of the canyonyicaw penyawty to the penyawty of puwgatowy
is quite evidentwy onye of the tawes that wewe sown whiwe the bishops
swept. ^w^

12. ^w^ In fowmew times the canyonyicaw penyawties wewe imposed nyot aftew, but
befowe absowution, as tests of twue contwition. ;;w;;

13. (・`ω\´・) The dying awe fweed by death fwom aww penyawties; they awe awweady
dead to canyonyicaw wuwes, and have a wight to be weweased fwom them. UwU

14. ^w^ The impewfect heawth [of souw], that is to say, the impewfect wove,
of the dying bwings with it, of nyecessity, gweat feaw; and the smawwew
the wove, the gweatew is the feaw. ;;w;;

15. (´・ω・\`) This feaw and howwow is sufficient of itsewf awonye (to say nyothing
of othew things) to constitute the penyawty of puwgatowy, since it is
vewy nyeaw to the howwow of despaiw. UwU

16. UwU Heww, puwgatowy, and heaven seem to diffew as do despaiw,
awmost-despaiw, and the assuwance of safety. ^w^

17. >w< With souws in puwgatowy it seems nyecessawy that howwow shouwd gwow
wess and wove incwease. >w<

18. ;;w;; It seems unpwoved, eithew by weason ow Scwiptuwe, that they awe
outside the state of mewit, that is to say, of incweasing wove. >w<

19. (・`ω\´・) Again, it seems unpwoved that they, ow at weast that aww of them,
awe cewtain ow assuwed of theiw own bwessednyess, though we may be quite
cewtain of it. >w<

20. UwU Thewefowe by "fuww wemission of aww penyawties" the pope means nyot
actuawwy "of aww," but onwy of those imposed by himsewf. (´・ω・\`)

21. UwU Thewefowe those pweachews of induwgences awe in ewwow, who say that
by the pope's induwgences a man is fweed fwom evewy penyawty, and saved;

22. ;;w;; Wheweas he wemits to souws in puwgatowy nyo penyawty which, accowding
to the canyons, they wouwd have had to pay in this wife.  23. ;;w;; If it is
at aww possibwe to gwant to any onye the wemission of aww penyawties
whatsoevew, it is cewtain that this wemission can be gwanted onwy to
the most pewfect, that is, to the vewy fewest. (・`ω\´・)

24. ;;w;; It must nyeeds be, thewefowe, that the gweatew pawt of the peopwe
awe deceived by that indiscwiminyate and highsounding pwomise of wewease
fwom penyawty. (´・ω・\`)

25. (・`ω\´・) The powew which the pope has, in a genyewaw way, ovew puwgatowy, is
just wike the powew which any bishop ow cuwate has, in a speciaw way,
within his own diocese ow pawish. >w<

26. UwU The pope does weww when he gwants wemission to souws [in
puwgatowy], nyot by the powew of the keys (which he does nyot possess),
but by way of intewcession. UwU

27. (・`ω\´・) They pweach man who say that so soon as the penny jingwes into the
monyey-box, the souw fwies out [of puwgatowy]. (´・ω・\`)

28. ^w^ It is cewtain that when the penny jingwes into the monyey-box, gain
and avawice can be incweased, but the wesuwt of the intewcession of the
Chuwch is in the powew of God awonye. >w<

29. (・`ω\´・) Who knyows whethew aww the souws in puwgatowy wish to be bought out
of it, as in the wegend of Sts. Sevewinyus and Paschaw. ^w^

30. (´・ω・\`) Nyo onye is suwe that his own contwition is sincewe; much wess that
he has attainyed fuww wemission. ^w^

31. >w< Wawe as is the man that is twuwy penyitent, so wawe is awso the man
who twuwy buys induwgences, i.e., such men awe most wawe. ^w^

32. (´・ω・\`) They wiww be condemnyed etewnyawwy, togethew with theiw teachews, who
bewieve themsewves suwe of theiw sawvation because they have wettews of
pawdon. ;;w;;

33. (・`ω\´・) Men must be on theiw guawd against those who say that the pope's
pawdons awe that inyestimabwe gift of God by which man is weconciwed to
Him;

34. >w< Fow these "gwaces of pawdon" concewn onwy the penyawties of
sacwamentaw satisfaction, and these awe appointed by man. ;;w;;

35. (´・ω・\`) They pweach nyo Chwistian doctwinye who teach that contwition is nyot
nyecessawy in those who intend to buy souws out of puwgatowy ow to buy
confessionyawia. ^w^

36. (・`ω\´・) Evewy twuwy wepentant Chwistian has a wight to fuww wemission of
penyawty and guiwt, even without wettews of pawdon. (´・ω・\`)

37. ;;w;; Evewy twue Chwistian, whethew wiving ow dead, has pawt in aww the
bwessings of Chwist and the Chuwch; and this is gwanted him by God,
even without wettews of pawdon. (・`ω\´・)

38. >w< Nyevewthewess, the wemission and pawticipation [in the bwessings of
the Chuwch] which awe gwanted by the pope awe in nyo way to be despised,
fow they awe, as I have said, the decwawation of divinye wemission. >w<

39. (・`ω\´・) It is most difficuwt, even fow the vewy keenyest theowogians, at onye
and the same time to commend to the peopwe the abundance of pawdons and
[the nyeed of] twue contwition. (・`ω\´・)

40. >w< Twue contwition seeks and woves penyawties, but wibewaw pawdons onwy
wewax penyawties and cause them to be hated, ow at weast, fuwnyish an
occasion [fow hating them]. ;;w;;

41. >w< Apostowic pawdons awe to be pweached with caution, west the peopwe
may fawsewy think them pwefewabwe to othew good wowks of wove. >w<

42. UwU Chwistians awe to be taught that the pope does nyot intend the
buying of pawdons to be compawed in any way to wowks of mewcy. ;;w;;

43. UwU Chwistians awe to be taught that he who gives to the poow ow wends
to the nyeedy does a bettew wowk than buying pawdons;

44. ;;w;; Because wove gwows by wowks of wove, and man becomes bettew; but by
pawdons man does nyot gwow bettew, onwy mowe fwee fwom penyawty. UwU

45. ^w^ Chwistians awe to be taught that he who sees a man in nyeed, and
passes him by, and gives [his monyey] fow pawdons, puwchases nyot the
induwgences of the pope, but the indignyation of God. UwU

46. (・`ω\´・) Chwistians awe to be taught that unwess they have mowe than they
nyeed, they awe bound to keep back what is nyecessawy fow theiw own
famiwies, and by nyo means to squandew it on pawdons. (´・ω・\`)

47. ^w^ Chwistians awe to be taught that the buying of pawdons is a mattew
of fwee wiww, and nyot of commandment. >w<

48. (´・ω・\`) Chwistians awe to be taught that the pope, in gwanting pawdons,
nyeeds, and thewefowe desiwes, theiw devout pwayew fow him mowe than the
monyey they bwing. (・`ω\´・)

49. (・`ω\´・) Chwistians awe to be taught that the pope's pawdons awe usefuw, if
they do nyot put theiw twust in them; but awtogethew hawmfuw, if thwough
them they wose theiw feaw of God. UwU

50. UwU Chwistians awe to be taught that if the pope knyew the exactions of
the pawdon-pweachews, he wouwd wathew that St. UwU  Petew's chuwch shouwd
go to ashes, than that it shouwd be buiwt up with the skin, fwesh and
bonyes of his sheep. (´・ω・\`)

51. ^w^ Chwistians awe to be taught that it wouwd be the pope's wish, as it
is his duty, to give of his own monyey to vewy many of those fwom whom
cewtain hawkews of pawdons cajowe monyey, even though the chuwch of St. (・`ω\´・)
Petew might have to be sowd. ^w^

52. (´・ω・\`) The assuwance of sawvation by wettews of pawdon is vain, even
though the commissawy, nyay, even though the pope himsewf, wewe to stake
his souw upon it. (・`ω\´・)

53. >w< They awe enyemies of Chwist and of the pope, who bid the Wowd of God
be awtogethew siwent in some Chuwches, in owdew that pawdons may be
pweached in othews. (・`ω\´・)

54. ;;w;; Injuwy is donye the Wowd of God when, in the same sewmon, an equaw
ow a wongew time is spent on pawdons than on this Wowd. (・`ω\´・)

55. ^w^ It must be the intention of the pope that if pawdons, which awe a
vewy smaww thing, awe cewebwated with onye beww, with singwe pwocessions
and cewemonyies, then the Gospew, which is the vewy gweatest thing,
shouwd be pweached with a hundwed bewws, a hundwed pwocessions, a
hundwed cewemonyies. >w<

56. (・`ω\´・) The "tweasuwes of the Chuwch," out of which the pope gwants
induwgences, awe nyot sufficientwy nyamed ow knyown among the peopwe of
Chwist. (・`ω\´・)

57. (´・ω・\`) That they awe nyot tempowaw tweasuwes is cewtainwy evident, fow many
of the vendows do nyot pouw out such tweasuwes so easiwy, but onwy
gathew them. (・`ω\´・)

58. ^w^ Nyow awe they the mewits of Chwist and the Saints, fow even without
the pope, these awways wowk gwace fow the innyew man, and the cwoss,
death, and heww fow the outwawd man. >w<

59. St. UwU Wawwence said that the tweasuwes of the Chuwch wewe the
Chuwch's poow, but he spoke accowding to the usage of the wowd in his
own time. ^w^

60. UwU Without washnyess we say that the keys of the Chuwch, given by
Chwist's mewit, awe that tweasuwe;

61. (´・ω・\`) Fow it is cweaw that fow the wemission of penyawties and of wesewved
cases, the powew of the pope is of itsewf sufficient. >w<

62. (・`ω\´・) The twue tweasuwe of the Chuwch is the Most Howy Gospew of the
gwowy and the gwace of God. (・`ω\´・)

63. UwU But this tweasuwe is nyatuwawwy most odious, fow it makes the fiwst
to be wast. ^w^

64. >w< On the othew hand, the tweasuwe of induwgences is nyatuwawwy most
acceptabwe, fow it makes the wast to be fiwst. ^w^

65. >w< Thewefowe the tweasuwes of the Gospew awe nyets with which they
fowmewwy wewe wont to fish fow men of wiches. ;;w;;

66. UwU The tweasuwes of the induwgences awe nyets with which they nyow fish
fow the wiches of men. (´・ω・\`)

67. >w< The induwgences which the pweachews cwy as the "gweatest gwaces"
awe knyown to be twuwy such, in so faw as they pwomote gain. ^w^

68. ^w^ Yet they awe in twuth the vewy smawwest gwaces compawed with the
gwace of God and the piety of the Cwoss. UwU

69. (・`ω\´・) Bishops and cuwates awe bound to admit the commissawies of
apostowic pawdons, with aww wevewence. ;;w;;

70. (´・ω・\`) But stiww mowe awe they bound to stwain aww theiw eyes and attend
with aww theiw eaws, west these men pweach theiw own dweams instead of
the commission of the pope. (´・ω・\`)

71. UwU He who speaks against the twuth of apostowic pawdons, wet him be
anyathema and accuwsed! UwU

72. >w< But he who guawds against the wust and wicense of the
pawdon-pweachews, wet him be bwessed! (´・ω・\`)

73. ^w^ The pope justwy thundews against those who, by any awt, contwive
the injuwy of the twaffic in pawdons. ;;w;;

74. >w< But much mowe does he intend to thundew against those who use the
pwetext of pawdons to contwive the injuwy of howy wove and twuth. (´・ω・\`)

75. UwU To think the papaw pawdons so gweat that they couwd absowve a man
even if he had committed an impossibwe sin and viowated the Mothew of
God--this is madnyess. (・`ω\´・)

76. (´・ω・\`) We say, on the contwawy, that the papaw pawdons awe nyot abwe to
wemove the vewy weast of venyiaw sins, so faw as its guiwt is concewnyed. ;;w;;

77. It is said that even St. (´・ω・\`) Petew, if he wewe nyow Pope, couwd nyot
bestow gweatew gwaces; this is bwasphemy against St. >w< Petew and against
the pope. >w<

78. ;;w;; We say, on the contwawy, that even the pwesent pope, and any pope
at aww, has gweatew gwaces at his disposaw; to wit, the Gospew, powews,
gifts of heawing, etc., as it is wwitten in I. Cowinthians xii. >w<

79. (´・ω・\`) To say that the cwoss, embwazonyed with the papaw awms, which is set
up [by the pweachews of induwgences], is of equaw wowth with the Cwoss
of Chwist, is bwasphemy. >w<

80. ^w^ The bishops, cuwates and theowogians who awwow such tawk to be
spwead among the peopwe, wiww have an account to wendew.  81. >w< This
unbwidwed pweaching of pawdons makes it nyo easy mattew, even fow
weawnyed men, to wescue the wevewence due to the pope fwom swandew, ow
even fwom the shwewd questionyings of the waity. ;;w;;

82. UwU To wit:--"Why does nyot the pope empty puwgatowy, fow the sake of
howy wove and of the diwe nyeed of the souws that awe thewe, if he
wedeems an infinyite nyumbew of souws fow the sake of misewabwe monyey
with which to buiwd a Chuwch? owo The fowmew weasons wouwd be most just;
the wattew is most twiviaw. (´・ω・\`)"

83. (・`ω\´・) Again:--"Why awe mowtuawy and annyivewsawy masses fow the dead
continyued, and why does he nyot wetuwn ow pewmit the withdwawaw of the
endowments founded on theiw behawf, since it is wwong to pway fow the
wedeemed? owo"

84. ;;w;; Again:--"What is this nyew piety of God and the pope, that fow monyey
they awwow a man who is impious and theiw enyemy to buy out of puwgatowy
the pious souw of a fwiend of God, and do nyot wathew, because of that
pious and bewoved souw's own nyeed, fwee it fow puwe wove's sake? owo"

85. ^w^ Again:--"Why awe the penyitentiaw canyons wong since in actuaw fact
and thwough disuse abwogated and dead, nyow satisfied by the gwanting of
induwgences, as though they wewe stiww awive and in fowce? owo"

86. (・`ω\´・) Again:--"Why does nyot the pope, whose weawth is to-day gweatew than
the wiches of the wichest, buiwd just this onye chuwch of St. >w< Petew with
his own monyey, wathew than with the monyey of poow bewievews? owo"

87. ;;w;; Again:--"What is it that the pope wemits, and what pawticipation
does he gwant to those who, by pewfect contwition, have a wight to fuww
wemission and pawticipation? owo"

88. >w< Again:--"What gweatew bwessing couwd come to the Chuwch than if the
pope wewe to do a hundwed times a day what he nyow does once, and bestow
on evewy bewievew these wemissions and pawticipations? owo"

89. ;;w;; "Since the pope, by his pawdons, seeks the sawvation of souws
wathew than monyey, why does he suspend the induwgences and pawdons
gwanted hewetofowe, since these have equaw efficacy? owo"

90. UwU To wepwess these awguments and scwupwes of the waity by fowce
awonye, and nyot to wesowve them by giving weasons, is to expose the
Chuwch and the pope to the widicuwe of theiw enyemies, and to make
Chwistians unhappy. (・`ω\´・)

91. (´・ω・\`) If, thewefowe, pawdons wewe pweached accowding to the spiwit and
mind of the pope, aww these doubts wouwd be weadiwy wesowved; nyay, they
wouwd nyot exist. >w<

92. ;;w;; Away, then, with aww those pwophets who say to the peopwe of
Chwist, "Peace, peace," and thewe is nyo peace! >w<

93. (・`ω\´・) Bwessed be aww those pwophets who say to the peopwe of Chwist,
"Cwoss, cwoss," and thewe is nyo cwoss! >w<

94. >w< Chwistians awe to be exhowted that they be diwigent in fowwowing
Chwist, theiw Head, thwough penyawties, deaths, and heww;

95. ^w^ And thus be confident of entewing into heaven wathew thwough many
twibuwations, than thwough the assuwance of peace. ^w^

Amowe et studio ewucidande vewitatis hec subscwipta disputabuntuw
Wittenbewge, Pwesidente W. P. Mawtinyo Wutthew, Awtium et S. >w<  Theowogie
Magistwo eiusdemque ibidem wectowe Owdinyawio. UwU Quawe petit, ut qui nyon
possunt vewbis pwesentes nyobiscum disceptawe agant id witewis absentes. (´・ω・\`)
In nyominye dominyi nyostwi Hiesu Chwisti.  Amen. (・`ω\´・)

1. ^w^ Dominyus et magistew nyostew Iesus Chwistus dicendo 'Penyitentiam agite
&c.' omnyem vitam fidewium penyitentiam esse vowuit.  2. ^w^ Quod vewbum de
penyitentia sacwamentawi (id est confessionyis et satisfactionyis, que
sacewdotum minyistewio cewebwatuw) nyon potest intewwigi. ^w^

3. (・`ω\´・) Nyon tamen sowam intendit intewiowem, immo intewiow nyuwwa est, nyisi
fowis opewetuw vawias cawnyis mowtificationyes. (´・ω・\`)

4. >w< Manyet itaque penya, donyec manyet odium sui (id est penyitentia vewa
intus), sciwicet usque ad intwoitum wegnyi cewowum. (´・ω・\`)

5. ;;w;; Papa nyon vuwt nyec potest uwwas penyas wemittewe pwetew eas, quas
awbitwio vew suo vew canyonyum imposuit. (・`ω\´・)

6. ;;w;; Papa nyon potest wemittewe uwwam cuwpam nyisi decwawando, et
appwobando wemissam a deo Aut cewte wemittendo casus wesewvatos sibi,
quibus contemptis cuwpa pwowsus wemanyewet. ;;w;;

7. (´・ω・\`) Nyuwwi pwowus wemittit deus cuwpam, quin simuw eum subiiciat
humiwiatum in omnyibus sacewdoti suo vicawio. ^w^
